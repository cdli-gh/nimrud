Room Z

Room Z was a narrow passage that connected courtyard Y with the west wing of the palace. Only its eastern end has survived. Layard found here nine slabs (plus six in the doorways). Paley and Sobolewski point out that the designations on Layardâ€™s Plan III for this room are faulty: there is no Z-1, though it is mentioned in the text and there is no slab base in position Z-7, probably a door. Layard sent three complete reliefs (Z-1, Z-2, Z-a-1) and one figure from a fourth (Z-8) to the British Museum; three slabs (Z-4, Z-a-2, Z-b-1) are lost, and the bottom parts of two more (Z-5, Z-10) are still in situ.
